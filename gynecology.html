pre and post natal check up

Your GP or maternity care provider will check your blood pressure and weight and may listen to your heart and chest. If you're breastfeeding, you may have your breasts and nipples checked.

Gynecological diagnosis

A gynecologist diagnoses and treats issues with female reproductive organs. They deal with all aspects of sexual health like preventive care, cancer screenings and physical exams.

Surgery on Uterus

Hysterectomy is surgery to remove the uterus. After a hysterectomy, you will not menstruate (have periods) or be able to get pregnant. Uterus removal is a common treatment for a variety of conditions that affect a woman's reproductive organs.

infertility treatment

Most of the time, this diagnosis can be made based on your history, physical exam and lab tests. Other times, a biopsy may be done to find conditions that contribute to infertility. Or it may be used to collect sperm for assisted reproductive techniques, such as in vitro fertilization (IVF).

Cervix

The lower, narrow end of the uterus (womb) that connects the uterus to the vagina (birth canal). The cervix allows fluids, such as menstrual blood, to pass from the uterus into the vagina. It also widens during the birth of a baby.

Laparoscopic hysterectomy

Laparoscopic hysterectomy is a vaginal hysterectomy performed with the aid of a laparoscope, a thin, flexible tube containing a video camera. Thin tubes are inserted through tiny incisions in the abdomen near the navel.

Laparoscopic Tubal Ligation

What is a laparoscopic bilateral tubal ligation? Tubal ligation is a surgical procedure that creates permanent contraception, or sterilization. It is commonly referred to as having your “tubes tied.

Laparoscopic Ophorectomy

A laparoscopic oophorectomy. Some women have a family history of ovarian cancer so removing their ovaries will remove the risk of developing ovarian cancer.

Normal Delivery

The first stage of the normal delivery process involves contractions which help the cervix dilate, soften and stretch so that the baby can be delivered.

caesarean section

Cesarean section, C-section, or cesarean birth is the surgical delivery of a baby through a cut (incision) made in the birth parent's abdomen and uterus.
